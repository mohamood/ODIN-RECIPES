SOMALI SABAYAD RECIPE


Soft crispy traditional somali chapati that can be substituted for bread

Sabaayad is a Somali flatbread made from flour. It is golden brown, crispy and flaky on the outside and soft on the inside.

You can eat it with almost anything: honey, cinnamon sugar and a cup of tea, stew, sauce or by itself

Ingredients

  • All purpose flour
  • Sugar
  • Salt
  • Oil
  • Hot water

STEPS

  1. Put 3cups of all purpose flour in bowl
  2. Add 4table spoons of sugar in the flour
  3. add half teaspoon of salt
  4. mix the dry mixture very well with wisk
  5. Slowly add boiling hot water and mix slowly until you form dough
  6. you can use dough mixer if you prefer
  7. after making the dough leave it to rest for 30min
  8. Cut the dough into smaller sized pieces
  9. Roll out 1 piece into a circle on a lightly floured work surface. Brush the top of the dough evenly with oil.
  10. Fold sides in to meet in the center. Fold top and bottom edges to meet in the center to make a square shape.
  11. repeat the same process for the rest of pieces
  12. Preheat a large skillet/pan over medium heat.
  13. Roll 1 square of dough into a circle again on a lightly floured work surface.
    Place in the skillet and cook until slightly puffed, 1 to 2 minutes.
    Drizzle a little bit of oil on top and flip;
    continue cooking until golden brown and crispy, 1 to 2 minutes more.
  14. Transfer to a plate and cover with a clean kitchen towel to keep sabaayad warm and soft. Repeat with remaining pieces.
